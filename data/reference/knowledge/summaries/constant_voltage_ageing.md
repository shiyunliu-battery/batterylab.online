# Constant Voltage Ageing — Handbook Summary

## Primary Planning Role

- **This chapter is outline-only (p. 62).** No executable procedure is fully defined in the extracted text.
- Do not use this source alone to draft a runnable protocol. A reviewed SOP or a stronger procedure from complementary literature must be attached before release.
- The method is relevant for studying lithium plating risk, separator stress, and electrolyte degradation under sustained high-voltage hold.

## Chapter Outline (p. 62)

- The chapter identifies constant-voltage hold as a distinct ageing mode from rest or cycle ageing.
- A constant voltage is maintained at the cell terminals over an extended period.
- Current decay during the hold and periodic capacity check-ups are the primary observables.
- The chapter does not specify voltage levels, hold durations, or check-up cadence — these must come from a project-specific SOP.

## Planner Notes

- When planning this test, the following must be defined and reviewed before release:
  - Target hold voltage (within the cell's approved voltage limits)
  - Hold duration and periodicity
  - Check-up protocol (capacity, resistance) and cadence
  - Safety abort rules for current, temperature, or gas detection
- EIS before and after the CV hold is a recommended optional extension for degradation attribution.
- This method is **not execution-ready** from the handbook alone. Do not present it as a drill-down from the core method reference set without a reviewed SOP.
