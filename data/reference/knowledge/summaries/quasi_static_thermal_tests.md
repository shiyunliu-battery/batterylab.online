# Quasi-Static Thermal Tests — Handbook Summary

## Primary Planning Role

- Use this chapter as the reference for synchronised swelling / dilatometry paired with an electrical capacity-style cycle.
- The mechanical measurement (thickness, pressure, strain) must be synchronised with the electrical measurement — this coupling is the core of the method.
- Do not treat this as a generic thermal test; the chapter specifically covers swelling and mechanical response.

## Locked Core Elements

- Cell mounted in a dilatometer, compression plate, or pressure-sensing jig.
- Paired slow or 1C electrical charge–discharge cycle (same profile family as the standard cycle or capacity test).
- Synchronised thickness / pressure / strain data at the same cadence as voltage and current.
- Baseline at 25 ± 2 °C; temperature extension requires reviewed conditions.

## Key Source Facts (pp. 47–48)

- Swelling is correlated with lithiation / delithiation of the anode (graphite expansion) and cathode (NMC contraction).
- The test can detect non-linear swelling events that correlate with ageing mechanisms such as lithium plating or SEI growth.
- Equipment: dilatometer or compression plate with force/displacement sensor, battery cell tester, temperature sensors.

## Planner Notes

- Fixture pre-load matters: define and log the initial compression force before cycling begins.
- This method is most useful at BOL and after ageing checkpoints to track swelling evolution.
- Combine with the capacity test or slow-curve cycle to generate multi-channel (electrical + mechanical) datasets.
