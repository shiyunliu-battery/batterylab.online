# Thermal Impedance Test — Handbook Summary

## Primary Planning Role

- Use this chapter as the primary reference for sinusoidal thermal-impedance measurement and parameter fitting.
- Keep the 25 °C stabilisation, thermostated-plate fixture, and 0.1–10 mHz frequency window explicit in any plan.
- This method is complementary to the thermal characterisation method (pp. 38–39).

## Locked Core Elements

- Cell stabilised at 25 ± 2 °C with thermostated plate before the sweep.
- Sinusoidal heat excitation applied via the electrical current or a separate heater element.
- Frequency sweep: 0.1 mHz to 10 mHz (thermal frequency domain, not electrical).
- Thermal impedance magnitude and phase measured at each frequency.
- Fit a thermal RC equivalent circuit to extract thermal resistance and capacitance.

## Key Source Facts (pp. 44–46)

- The thermal impedance spectra are analogous to EIS but probe the thermal rather than electrochemical network.
- At low frequency the spectrum approaches the steady-state thermal resistance; at high frequency the thermal capacitance dominates.
- The method enables thermal model parameter identification for BMS and cooling-system design.
- Equipment: precision current source or heater with controllable sinusoidal excitation, thermocouple array, data logger.

## Planner Notes

- The thermal frequency window (0.1–10 mHz) corresponds to very long sampling periods (100–10000 s per half-cycle). Plan acquisition time accordingly.
- Ensure the cell is at thermal steady state at 25 °C before starting the sweep; any residual transient from previous electrical tests will corrupt the lowest-frequency data points.
