# Thermal Characterisation — Handbook Summary

## Primary Planning Role

- Use this chapter as the reference for pseudo-adiabatic and thermostated-plate thermal mapping.
- The two-stage thermal fixture logic (pseudo-adiabatic enclosure first, then thermostated plate) must remain explicit even when the exact current profile is tailored.
- Do not substitute generic thermal measurements for this method; the fixture discipline is the core of the method.

## Locked Core Elements

- **Stage 1 — Pseudo-adiabatic**: Insulate the cell and discharge at selected C-rate; record temperature rise as a proxy for heat generation.
- **Stage 2 — Thermostated plate**: Attach cell to a temperature-controlled plate and measure the thermal resistance from cell to heat sink.
- Temperature sensors must cover both the hottest cell region and the fixture reference.
- Baseline at 25 ± 2 °C; multi-temperature extensions require separate reviewed conditions.
- Logging: temperature and current at a cadence fine enough to resolve transient heat pulses.

## Key Source Facts (pp. 38–39)

- The chapter gives methods to measure heat generation rate and thermal resistance separately.
- The pseudo-adiabatic approach measures the combined loss: ohmic, charge-transfer, and entropic.
- The thermostated-plate approach measures the thermal resistance from cell body to the contact face.
- Both are needed for a complete thermal model parameterisation.

## Planner Notes

- This method is intended to feed thermal model parameters, not to characterise electrical performance.
- Pair with the thermal impedance test (pp. 44–46) if a frequency-resolved thermal impedance is also needed.
- The exact C-rate sequence for the pseudo-adiabatic stage is review-controlled but must match the application-representative rate.
